## Narrative Order

The default candidate story uses four projects:

1. Opportunity Workbench for discovery and qualification.
2. MLOps Platform for technical infrastructure depth.
3. Capacity Planner for first-pass workload and commercial sizing.
4. TCO Workbench for transparent value engineering.

GCP Data, Agentic FP&A, and the Solution Configurator provide supporting evidence for cloud, governance, enterprise AI controls, and architecture workshops.

## Public Boundaries

- Foundation repositories are sanitized blueprints and do not provide complete deployable environments.
- Commercial applications are local, single-user demonstrations without production identity, tenancy, central audit retention, or approved data-classification workflows.
- Capacity outputs require representative workload benchmarks and formal Solutions Engineer validation.
- Configurator outputs are initial solution hypotheses, not final architectures or bills of materials.
- TCO outputs use fictional or user-entered assumptions and are not live quotes, financial advice, or guaranteed outcomes.

## Maintenance Checklist

Before changing claims on the landing page:

1. Verify the default branch and current README of the linked repository.
2. Confirm test, coverage, browser, and container figures against the latest successful workflow.
3. Keep fictional scenario values separate from professional experience results.
4. Check local Markdown links and parse every SVG.
5. Review mobile readability of diagrams and the landing-page word count.
6. Preserve the distinction between blueprint, prototype, benchmark, quote, and production deployment.
